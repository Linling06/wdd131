What Is Astronomy?

Astronomy is the study of everything beyond the Earth’s atmosphere, including planets, moons, stars, nebulae, and galaxies. It helps us understand where we are in the universe and how it works.

On SpaceExplorer, you will find short explanations, simple diagrams, and clear language meant for curious beginners.

Black Holes: Gravity’s Giants

Black holes are regions of space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They form when very massive stars collapse at the end of their lives.

Astronomers cannot see black holes directly, but they observe how nearby stars and gas move and how light is bent around them. Studying black holes helps scientists test ideas about gravity, time, and the limits of our universe.

Galaxies: Island Universes

A galaxy is a huge collection of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter all held together by gravity. Our home, the Milky Way, is just one of billions of galaxies in the observable universe.

Some galaxies are graceful spirals, others look like fuzzy balls or strange, distorted shapes. By exploring galaxies, we learn how stars are born and die and how the universe has changed over billions of years.
